Hi, I'm Ali Seyhun.

I'm a Data and Behavioral Scientist working at the intersection of human behavior and data-driven decision-making, currently at the CEB Secretariat, United Nations Common System.

As an economist with a data science and software developer skill set, I build statistical models for analysis and prediction, develop data pipelines, software, and dashboards, create machine-learning and LLM-supported tools, and design causal inference strategies and experiments to understand human behavior and inform policy.

I come from an academic background. I hold a PhD in Economics and Management from the University of Trento, Italy. After my doctorate, I worked as a researcher and lab manager at institutions such as the Max Planck Institute, the University of Bologna, and the Institute for Advanced Study in Toulouse. (See Experience section for details.)

I have an interdisciplinary approach to human behavior and applications of its reseach on industry and policy. For my research, I had the opportunity to collaborate with brilliant researchers from various disciplines, including economists, psychologists, anthropologists, biologists, computer scientists, and mathematicians. Together with my co-authors, I have published work on cooperation, reciprocity, group decision-making, social learning, cultural evolution, and the economics of inequality and migration, on various impactful multidisciplinary and specialized journals (See Research section for details.)

I am an experimentalist by training (and by heart) but my work draws on methods from various fields such as microeconomics, applied economics, causal inference, game theory, evolutionary biology, agent-based modeling, and social choice theory.
