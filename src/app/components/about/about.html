Hi, I'm Ali Seyhun.

I'm a Data and Behavioral Scientist working at the intersection of human behavior and data-driven decision-making, currently at the CEB Secretariat, United Nations Common System. An economist with a data science and software developer skill set, I build statistical models for analysis and prediction, develop data pipelines, software, and dashboards, create machine-learning and LLM-supported tools, and design causal inference strategies and experiments to understand human behavior and inform policy.

I have an academic background: I hold a PhD in Economics and Management from the University of Trento, Italy. After my doctorate, I worked as a researcher and lab manager at institutions such as the Max Planck Institute for Research on Collective Goods (now Max Planck Institute for Behavioral Economics), University of Bologna, and the Institute for Advanced Study in Toulouse. (See Experience section for details.)

I take an interdisciplinary approach to human behavior and its applications in industry and policy. I see people's choices as shaped by the interplay of economic incentives, social preferences, and cultural and social norms, and I work with data on people and their decisions to bring these many pieces together.

To study these forces, I am an experimentalist by training (and by heart), but my work draws on methods from various fields, including microeconomics, applied economics, causal inference, game theory, evolutionary biology, agent-based modeling, and social choice theory.

For my research, I have had the opportunity to collaborate with brilliant researchers across disciplines, including economists, psychologists, anthropologists, biologists, computer scientists, and mathematicians. Together with my co-authors, I have published a wide range of scientific work on cooperation, reciprocity, group decision-making, social learning, cultural evolution, and the economics of inequality and migration, in impactful field-specific and interdisciplinary journals. (See Research section for details.)
